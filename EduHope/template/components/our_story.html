Our Story

How it all began and the journey so far

2010

The Beginning

EduHope was founded by Maria Rodriguez, a former teacher who witnessed firsthand the barriers preventing children in rural communities from accessing quality education. With a small group of volunteers and a passionate vision, she established our first learning center in a remote village.

2012

First Expansion

After proving our model's effectiveness in the initial community, we expanded to five additional villages. We partnered with local governments to train teachers and establish sustainable educational programs.

2015

Digital Learning Initiative

Recognizing the importance of technology in modern education, we launched our Digital Learning Initiative, bringing tablets and internet connectivity to remote classrooms. This program significantly improved student engagement and learning outcomes.

2018

International Recognition

EduHope received the Global Education Innovation Award for our work in bridging the educational gap in underserved communities. This recognition helped us secure partnerships with international organizations and expand our reach.

2020

Pandemic Response

During the global pandemic, we quickly adapted our programs to provide remote learning support and distribute educational materials to families in need. Our emergency response reached over 15,000 students across 50 communities.

Today

Looking Forward

Today, EduHope operates in 15 countries, serving over 10,000 students annually. We continue to innovate and expand our programs, with plans to reach 50,000 students by 2025 through our new "Education for All" initiative.
